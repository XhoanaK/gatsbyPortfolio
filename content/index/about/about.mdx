My name is Xhoana Koca (the "Xh" is pronounced like a "J"). I grew up in Korçë, Albania — curious about technology long before I had reliable access to it. When my family got our first computer in 2009, I became the household's go-to tech person. At 12, my family and I moved to the United States, a transition that shaped both my resilience and my ability to communicate across cultures and teams.

I graduated Magna Cum Laude from New York City College of Technology (CUNY) with a Bachelor of Technology in Computer Systems Technology — GPA 3.54, Dean's List four semesters.

Over the past 4+ years I've worked as a Business Analyst and QA Analyst, most recently at Citi Group, supporting enterprise software delivery across global teams. My work has included facilitating UAT, SIT, and regression testing cycles; managing 30+ weekly incidents in JIRA and ServiceNow; coordinating 10+ production releases in Agile sprints; and building Excel dashboards tracking KPIs and defect trends across 150+ issues. I've collaborated with developers, product owners, vendors, and stakeholders across NA, EU, and APAC regions.

I bridge the gap between technical and business teams — fluent enough in technology to understand system constraints, and clear enough in communication to turn complex requirements into actionable documentation and solutions.
